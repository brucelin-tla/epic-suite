## Key findings — Dan Martell (Buy Back Your Time / danmartell.com)
- **Buyback Loop** (Audit → Transfer → Fill) and the **DRIP Matrix** (sort tasks by financial value × energy drain into Delegate / Replace / Invest / Produce) are his core prioritization tools for what to automate first. [buybackyourtime.com; risewithdrew.com/the-buyback-loop-transforming-time-into-profit-for-entrepreneurs]
- **Replacement Ladder**: hire/automate in order **Admin → Delivery → Marketing → Sales → Leadership** — admin/inbox/calendar work goes first, sales capability comes later. [risewithdrew.com/dan-martells-replacement-ladder-how-to-buy-back-my-time-so-i-can-focus-on-what-energizes-me; danmartell LinkedIn]
- His named "AI agent" categories for a business include an **Assistant agent** (admin/email/calendar — same tier as the Replacement Ladder's first hire) and a **Closer agent** (lead qualification/follow-up, with him explicitly bullish that AI can absorb real chunks of sales-qualification work). [danmartell.com/how-to-get-rich-with-ai-agents-0-to-1m-guide]
- Stated view on why founders stall: **"the biggest risk of any business is building something a customer doesn't want"** — he criticizes founders who "hide behind their data" (watching dashboards from the back room instead of engaging customers) instead of selling/validating directly. [leanb2bbook.com/blog/dan-martell-product-engagement] *(paraphrase — exact "building for the sake of building" phrase not independently verified, but this is the consistent, on-record equivalent theme.)*

## Key findings — Alex Hormozi ($100M Offers / $100M Leads)
- **Value Equation**: Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice). His stated advice: competitors push the numerator (bigger promises); the fastest differentiation is attacking the denominator, since almost nobody optimizes time delay or effort. [gregfaxon.com/blog/100m-offers-summary; creatoreconomy.so/p/the-value-equation-for-irresistible-products]
- **Core Four** lead channels: warm outreach, cold outreach, content, paid ads — warm/referral leads already carry trust and should skip straight to the core pitch; his explicit sequence includes ending warm-outreach conversations by **asking directly for referrals**. [shortform.com/blog/100m-leads-alex-hormozi; ahamastery.com/referral-marketing-the-alex-hormozi-playbook]
- Referral asks work best captured at the customer's **"success moment"** (right after they've gotten a win), not at arbitrary times. [andrejthefreelancer.medium.com/referral-playbook-ee81235d1929]
- **CLOSER call framework**: Clarify → Label → Overview (past failed attempts, builds urgency) → Sell the outcome not the mechanism → Explain/handle objections → Reinforce the decision post-sale. **3 A's** for objections: Acknowledge → Associate with a past success story → Ask confidently for the sale. [skool.com/acceleratoruniversity/what-is-alex-hormozis-closer-framework; jointhefollowup.com/p/hormozis-3-a-sales-framework]
- Retention compounds in a way acquisition doesn't; he frames referral-rate vs. churn-rate as the structural lever for growth without added ad spend. *(Specific LTV:CAC ratio numbers circulating in secondary sources could not be verified against a primary source — treat as paraphrase, not a citable stat.)* [blog.forecastingperformance.com/p/hormozis-favorite-analysis]

## Concrete next steps for EPIC Suite (ranked by what most directly speeds up closing real calls TODAY)
1. **Promote beta → live this week.** This is the single highest-leverage action available and it's pure Buyback-Loop "Production" work sitting idle: real auth, real Sales Hub leads, real commission tracking, real Stripe confirmation, and the real Fathom call-data pipeline are all built and just not in front of the team running real calls. Per Martell's own framing, shipping validated work beats building more of it. Per Hormozi's Value Equation, every day this stays in beta, real prospects experience *more* time-delay/effort (manual booking, no live paid-confirmation) than the already-built version requires — that's a live conversion tax with a known fix sitting in the repo.
2. **Add a scripted referral ask to Client Success at the onboarding "success moment."** Nothing in `success/index.html` currently prompts a member for a referral right after they onboard — Hormozi's Core Four explicitly calls this the highest-trust, cheapest channel, and it's the exact success-moment trigger his referral playbook calls out. This is a small, concrete content/UI addition (one card/prompt), not a new system — TLA already has a full Referral Partner tool, this just points existing onboarding traffic into it at its most receptive moment.
3. **Layer the CLOSER/3-A's structure onto the Closer's real (non-training) live-call mode as on-screen cues**, not just in the scripted practice call — confirm whether the "plain live-call mode" for real Sales Hub leads (1.28.0) carries the same BANT dock/objection-search/Say-This cues the training mode has, and if not, wire it in. This is reusing what's already built (the objection bank, BANT dock) against real leads, not new build.
4. **Do NOT build real-time Twilio/Deepgram/Claude live-call transcription next**, even though the code comments flag it as "the real version." Per the Replacement Ladder, this is Sales-tier automation and should come *after* the Admin-tier wins (#1 above) are actually shipped and adopted — and the Fathom async pipeline already delivers structured call data without it. Building this now would be exactly the "keep building instead of shipping/selling" pattern Martell warns against.

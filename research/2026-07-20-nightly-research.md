## Key findings

### Dan Martell — automation frameworks
- The **Buyback Loop** (Audit → Transfer → Fill): first audit where time/effort actually goes, then transfer the found tasks (delegate, automate, or eliminate), then refill the freed capacity with higher-value work. Audit comes *before* automation — you can't decide what to automate without first measuring where the bottleneck is. [Buy Back Your Time summary — Insight Books](https://insightbooks.app/books/buy-back-your-time); [Different Glasses summary](https://differentglasses.com/buy-back-your-time/)
- The **Buyback Rate**: a dollar figure for your own time, used to decide whether a task is worth transferring to someone/something cheaper than that rate and not dependent on your unique judgment. [hireinsouth.com summary](https://www.hireinsouth.com/post/buy-back-your-time-a-book-summary-of-dan-martells-take-on-reclaiming-freedom)
- On AI specifically: Martell's current guidance is to **find the single biggest operational bottleneck and automate that constraint first**, rather than automating broadly, and to build the system/process before adding headcount. Humans stay on judgment, leadership, care, and creativity — "you cannot teach someone to care." [danmartell.com](https://www.danmartell.com/ai-is-about-to-change-business-forever/); [Dan Martell LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/dmartell_6-weeks-ago-i-told-my-team-that-ai-needs-activity-7367071287015297026-OT0q)
- No source found for a specific "always automate before you delegate" ordering rule — the framework treats automate/delegate/eliminate as three transfer options chosen per-task, not a strict sequence. Flagging this so it isn't overstated.

### Alex Hormozi — sales/offer frameworks
- **CLOSER** (from *$100M Leads*): Clarify why they're here → Label the problem → Overview past attempts (and position your offer as the missing piece) → Sell the outcome, not the mechanism ("sell the vacation, not the plane") → Explain away lingering objections → Reinforce the decision they've already made emotionally. Built from running sales teams across Hormozi's portfolio and taught to 115+ reps. [salescoachpro.io](https://www.salescoachpro.io/blog/hormozi-closer-framework); [Hormozi on Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/ahormozi/videos/the-closer-frameworkc-clarify-why-theyre-herel-label-their-problemo-overview-the/1322943372778894/)
- **Value Equation** (from *$100M Offers*): Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice). Perceived likelihood — driven by proof, guarantees, and testimonials — matters as much as actual outcomes for the buying decision. [gregfaxon.com](https://www.gregfaxon.com/blog/100m-offers-summary); [creatoreconomy.so](https://creatoreconomy.so/p/the-value-equation-for-irresistible-products)
- **Core Four** (from *$100M Leads*): the only four lead-gen channels are warm outreach, cold outreach, free content, and paid ads. Recommended sequencing: start with warm outreach + content, add cold outreach, then paid ads once the offer and sales process are proven. [think7figures.com](https://think7figures.com/alex-hormozi-100m-leads-framework/); [Growth Experts](https://growthexperts.co/the-core-4/)

## Concrete next steps for EPIC Suite (ranked by what most directly speeds up closing real calls today)

1. **Instrument real funnel visibility in Admin before anything else** — a minimal, honest view of today's/this week's real call volume, bookings, and close rate, replacing (or clearly separating from) the demo-data section. This is the Martell "audit" step: right now nobody, including Bruce, can see whether the constraint on more closed deals is lead volume, booking rate, or in-call close rate — every other prioritization call in this list is a guess without it. Keep it small: a handful of real numbers, not a BI project (see scope-creep flag below).
2. **Verify AI-coach adoption, then fix the actual blocker.** Ask the reps directly: has each of them pasted a working Anthropic key? If most haven't, that "AI co-pilot" is a feature that exists in code but not in practice on real calls. The fix is either (a) a quick team rollout push (get every rep a key, confirm it's pasted) if it's pure friction, or (b) if Bruce wants it provisioned centrally, route it through a Supabase edge function (the project already has two) instead of distributing one shared raw key to every device — cheap either way, but figure out which problem it actually is before building anything.
3. **Make the Client Success → Closer handoff real**, at minimum: persist the 3-bureau-call and onboarding checklist to Supabase, and make "loop in the closer" send an actual notification (even a simple one) instead of a local toast. This sits right at the join between a completed qualifying call and a real closing opportunity — today a rep can genuinely believe a handoff happened when it didn't.
4. **Either populate or remove the "AI priority score" queue-sort promise** in Setter/Closer — a fake signal that reps might be trusting to decide who to call next is worse than no signal; this is a small, contained fix.
5. **Close the referral-table join gap** flagged in-code at `setter/index.html:253-255` so warm, vetted referrals actually surface in the calling queue — directly increases real dial-able leads with no new feature, just wiring an existing table in.

## Scope-creep flags

- **The clearest scope-creep signal tonight is the changelog itself**: the overwhelming majority of recent commits are on Epic Life Ledger (the owner-only wealth calculator), not the five apps that run actual sales calls. Ledger is real, sophisticated, carefully-verified work — but it is a step further removed from dialing and closing than even Setter/Closer polish would be. Per Bruce's own acknowledged pattern, continued heavy Ledger investment this week should be treated as a flag, not a default, unless there's a specific closing-call reason for it (e.g., a rep needs it live on a call this week).
- **Item 1 above (Admin metrics) is a build, and could sprawl.** The ask here is a handful of real numbers pulled from tables that already exist (`prospects`, `bookings`, `commissions`) — not a dashboard framework, drill-downs, or historical trend charts. Scope it to "today/this week, five numbers" and stop.
- **The AI co-pilot and Renee roleplay trainer are the most tempting things to keep improving** (more prompts, better roleplay realism, more coaching nuance) — but per finding #2, nobody has confirmed real reps are even using the existing version on real calls. Improving it further before confirming adoption is exactly the "build instead of sell" pattern this brief is watching for.
- **Lead generation itself (Core Four: warm outreach, cold outreach, content, paid ads) is not a codebase problem** — the repo correctly has no ad/outreach automation, and it shouldn't grow one. If lead volume turns out to be the actual bottleneck (see finding #1's dependency on the external Sales Hub CRM), the fix is outreach activity and ad spend, not a new feature in this repo. Watch for any future ask to "build a lead-gen module" inside EPIC Suite — that would be solving a sales-activity problem with more software.

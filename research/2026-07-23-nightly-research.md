### Dan Martell — Buyback Principle / automation frameworks

- The **Buyback Loop** (Audit → Transfer → Fill): audit your calendar/tasks for low-value, repeatable, energy-draining work, transfer it to a person, process, or tool, then fill the freed time with $10k/hr work. ([Shortform](https://www.shortform.com/summary/buy-back-your-time-vb13747-e-summary-dan-martell); [Teamly](https://www.teamly.com/blog/buy-back-your-time-by-dan-martell-chapter-2/))
- The **DRIP Matrix** categorizes tasks by financial value vs. energy drain into four quadrants — Delegate, Replace, Invest, Produce. Martell's guidance: automation/delegation targets the **Delegate quadrant first** — menial, repetitive, low-value, draining work — before touching higher-value "Produce"/"Invest" work. ([Reading­graphics](https://readingraphics.com/book-summary-buy-back-your-time/); [Baylor Keller Center](https://kellercenter.hankamer.baylor.edu/news/story/2026/insider-buy-back-your-time))
- The **Replacement Ladder** sequences delegation so founders replace themselves in the right order rather than hiring/automating randomly, starting with admin/scheduling before moving to higher-value responsibilities. ([Reading­graphics](https://readingraphics.com/book-summary-buy-back-your-time/))

**Applied to EPIC Suite:** manually typing call notes and hunting for the right rebuttal mid-call is textbook Delegate-quadrant work — repetitive, low-judgment, and it's the exact thing `runRealCall()` currently forces the closer to do by hand. Per the Replacement Ladder, this is a *higher*-priority automation target than anything in the Ledger, because it sits directly in the revenue-producing motion (a live sales call), not in illustration/back-office polish.

### Alex Hormozi — sales, offer, and retention frameworks

- **Value Equation** ($100M Offers): Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort). Most businesses over-invest in the numerator (bigger promises); the highest-leverage, least-copied lever is shrinking the denominator — making the path to the outcome faster and more effortless. ([SuperSummary](https://www.supersummary.com/100m-offers/summary/); [creatoreconomy.so](https://creatoreconomy.so/p/the-value-equation-for-irresistible-products))
- **CLOSER framework** for the sales call itself: Clarify why they're on the call → Label their problem → Overview past Pain → Sell the vacation (the outcome, not the mechanism) → Explain away concerns → Reinforce the decision post-sale. ([Sales Coach Pro](https://www.salescoachpro.io/blog/hormozi-closer-framework); [The Follow Up](https://www.jointhefollowup.com/p/alex-hormozis-closer-sales-framework))
- **AAA objection handling**: Acknowledge → Associate (a past client with the same objection who got results) → Ask for the sale again. Requires 3–5 memorized proof stories per objection type, not improvisation. ([Deciphr](https://www.deciphr.ai/podcast/i-taught-115-salesmen-my-closing-framework-ep-306); [salesfit.ai](https://salesfit.ai/blog/closer-framework-vs-human-centric-selling))
- **Core Four** lead gen ($100M Leads): warm outreach, content, cold outreach, paid ads — one-to-one vs. one-to-many, crossed with warm vs. cold audience. Warm outreach is the fastest, cheapest channel to compound early. ([founder-playbook SKILL.md](https://github.com/getagentseal/founder-playbook/blob/main/100m-leads/SKILL.md); [Think 7 Figures](https://think7figures.com/alex-hormozi-100m-leads-framework/))
- **Retention/"leaky bucket"**: growth without fixing churn is "scaling into a leaky bucket" — working hard to fill the top while an equal number leave the bottom. Fix unit economics and retention *before* spending more on acquisition. ([clv-calculator.com](https://www.clv-calculator.com/customer-retention/crm-clv/leaky-bucket-theory/); [LinkedIn/Advik Yadav](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/improving-customer-retention-insights-from-alex-hormozi-advik-yadav-nhpmc))

**Applied to EPIC Suite:** the static `OBJ_BANK` already encodes Hormozi's AAA structure (agree→reframe→story) almost verbatim — that groundwork is done and doesn't need to be redesigned. What's missing is the Value Equation's denominator: on a real call, a closer has to *manually recall which objection was just raised and go find the matching entry* — that's Effort the framework says to strip out, not Dream Outcome or Likelihood. Warm outreach (referrals) is TLA's structurally strongest Core Four channel and already has a dedicated, live Referral tool — protect and speed that pipeline before investing in new acquisition channels.

## Concrete next steps for EPIC Suite (ranked by what most directly speeds closing real calls today)

1. **Wire the existing objection bank + Anthropic API call into `runRealCall()`.** The AI wiring and the AAA-structured content already exist in the demo path — this is finishing a half-built feature, not new scope. Minimum viable version: a one-tap "what did they just say" quick-tag (even without full live transcription) that surfaces the matching AAA rebuttal instantly during a real call, closing the Value-Equation "Effort" gap identified above. This is the single highest-leverage change available, because it turns a tool that already works in demo into one that works in production.
2. **Audit the sign-in-gate race condition class of bug.** A blank white screen mid-call is worse than a missing feature — it can end a closer's ability to work a real lead entirely. Confirm v1.47.0's fix is fully hardened (add a regression check) before any further hub/auth changes.
3. **Do not start new Ledger work until the above two ship.** Per the Buyback Loop/Replacement Ladder, this is where Bruce's own time and the team's build capacity should go first — the Ledger is valuable but is a Section-3-of-the-sales-process tool, not the thing that determines whether a call closes.
4. **Once (1) ships, extend the same "reduce effort" lens to post-call logging** — the CLOSER framework's final step (Reinforce) and Won→commission flow are already live; check whether closing out a call today still requires manual re-entry of what was already said in the objection-tag stream from step 1, and remove that duplication if so.

## Scope-creep flags

- **The clearest scope-creep signal is already in the data, not speculative**: 20 consecutive releases (1.42.0–1.52.12) went into the owner-only Ledger while the Closer's real-call gap sat unaddressed for 16 days. This matches Bruce's known pattern exactly and should be named plainly: Ledger work, however good, is not sales-call-closing work.
- **Recommendation #1 above risks becoming its own scope-creep project if over-scoped.** Do NOT build a new custom live-transcription/speech-to-text pipeline — that's a multi-week infra project. The minimum viable version (manual one-tap quick-tag → existing AAA content, using the Anthropic call path that's already wired) delivers most of the value in a fraction of the time. Watch for the temptation to "do it properly" with full real-time transcription before shipping the cheap version.
- **Don't build a second call-recording/lead-sync pipeline.** Admin already has a live Fathom webhook syncing recordings into structured lead data — any new "capture what happened on the call" idea should extend that, not duplicate it.

## Key findings

### Dan Martell — automation/delegation frameworks
- **Buyback Principle / Buyback Loop**: use money to buy back time; the loop is *Audit → Transfer → Fill* — audit where your time actually goes, transfer the task to a person/process/tool, then fill the freed time with higher-value work. [Buy Back Your Time summary — Baylor Keller Center](https://kellercenter.hankamer.baylor.edu/news/story/2026/insider-buy-back-your-time)
- **DRIP Matrix**: sorts tasks by money-value vs. energy-drain into Delegate / Replace / Invest / Produce; tasks that are low-money **and** low-energy ("Drain") should be deleted or delegated fastest. [Buy Back Your Time chapter summary — Teamly](https://www.teamly.com/blog/buy-back-your-time-by-dan-martell-chapter-2/)
- **Replacement Ladder**: the recommended delegation order is **Admin → Delivery → Marketing → Sales → Leadership** — i.e. hand off administrative/data work first; sales is one of the *last* functions to hand off, not the first. [7 Golden Concepts from Dan Martell — donnapro.com](https://donnapro.com/delegation-leadership/buy-back-your-time/)
- **Buyback Rate**: calculate what your hour is worth; anything a system/person can do for less than that rate, and that doesn't require your unique judgment, should move off your plate. [Buy Back Your Time summary — mindmapai.app](https://mindmapai.app/mind-mapping/buy-back-your-time-by-dan-martell)
- General automation-strategy consensus (not Martell-specific but consistent with his framing): automate repetitive, low-value tasks — initial lead capture, data logging/CRM hygiene — while keeping detailed consultations and complex negotiation/closing human, since human touch there builds the trust that drives retention. [Human Touch vs. Automation — craigjohnsonr5.substack.com](https://craigjohnsonr5.substack.com/p/human-touch-vs-automation)

**Read-through for EPIC Suite**: the Replacement Ladder says *admin first, sales last*. That maps almost exactly onto what's broken today — the gaps below are overwhelmingly in the "admin" layer (data capture, routing, dashboards), not in anything that requires replacing a human closer's judgment. Martell's framework argues for fixing that layer before touching the sales-conversation layer at all.

### Alex Hormozi — sales/offer frameworks
- **Value Equation** (`$100M Offers`): `Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)`. Most operators only work the numerator; the bigger unlock is collapsing the denominator — reducing how long it takes a prospect to see proof and how much effort/friction stands between them and saying yes. [Value Equation explainer — creatoreconomy.so](https://creatoreconomy.so/p/the-value-equation-for-irresistible-products)
- **CLOSER framework** for the call itself: **C**larify why they're on the call, **L**abel their problem, **O**verview past pain, **S**ell the vacation (the outcome, not the mechanism), **E**xplain away concerns, **R**einforce the decision post-sale. [CLOSER framework — Sales Coach Pro](https://www.salescoachpro.io/blog/hormozi-closer-framework)
- **AAA objection handling**: **A**cknowledge ("I totally get that"), **A**ssociate (connect to a past client who felt the same and got the result), **A**sk for the sale again. The goal is to address the underlying belief behind an objection, not the surface excuse. [3 A's framework — jointhefollowup.com](https://www.jointhefollowup.com/p/hormozis-3-a-sales-framework)
- **$100M Leads — Core Four**: warm outreach, content, cold outreach, paid ads, in that recommended sequence — start warm, add cold outreach, add paid last once the offer/sales process is proven. Distinguishes *raw* leads from **engaged leads** (people who've actually shown interest); a lead that never gets engaged is worthless regardless of volume. [$100M Leads overview — Shortform](https://www.shortform.com/blog/100m-leads-alex-hormozi/)
- Retention corollary: the same content/education used to generate leads should also be used to retain and delight existing customers — the mechanism doesn't stop at the sale. [$100M Leads key takeaways — loxie.app](https://loxie.app/learnwithloxie/100m-leads)

**Read-through for EPIC Suite**: TLA's actual bottleneck per the code isn't "we need a smarter AI on the call" — it's that **engaged leads are being lost or going unlogged before or after the human conversation even happens.** Hormozi's own framing (leads are worthless if they don't become tracked, engaged, followed-up leads) points at the routing/persistence gaps, not the call-script gap.

## Concrete next steps for EPIC Suite (ranked by what most directly speeds up closing real calls TODAY)

1. **Make the Closer log every real call outcome, not just referral-sourced ones.** `live/index.html`'s outcome handler only writes a `call_logs`/`deals` row and triggers `close-deal` when the lead came from the warm-referral path (`REAL_DEALS[curId]` present); for the majority of leads — anything sourced from Sales Hub/ads — logging "🏆 Won" or any other disposition today only advances the in-memory queue and is gone on refresh. That means **most real deals closed through this tool right now have no audit trail and never trigger a commission record.** This is pure "Admin" per Martell's Replacement Ladder (fix admin first) and it's the single highest-leverage item here because it's not a feature gap, it's data actively being lost during real sales calls happening today. Bounded fix: extend the same write path used for the referral case to run for every disposition regardless of source.
2. **Route unvetted Referral Partner submissions into the Setter's queue.** Setter's own code comments admit warm-but-unvetted referrals live in a table that "isn't joined into this queue yet." Per Hormozi, an unengaged lead is a dead lead — right now a cold-submitted referral has no automated path to a human BDR at all. Join `referrals`/`deals` into the Setter query (mirroring how `live/index.html` already does this for the Closer side) or explicitly route them elsewhere; either way, close the gap that's currently dropping real inbound leads on the floor.
3. **Promote Admin's real-numbers build from beta to root.** Beta's Admin dashboard has already been hand-updated with real closed-deal figures; production Admin still shows a hardcoded "Demo data"-labeled funnel/commission dataset next to genuinely live payout and team panels. This is a promotion, not new engineering — it's the fastest possible fix on this list and it's what Bruce and the team are looking at to decide where to spend selling energy.
4. **Give the Ledger minimal persistence (save/reload one member's inputs).** It currently has zero `localStorage` and zero Supabase write-back beyond auth — every number resets on reload. The Ledger is the tool that visually makes the velocity-banking/cash-value case *during* a sales conversation (this is the Value Equation's "Time Delay" lever — proof needs to survive across a multi-call sales cycle without re-entering a prospect's mortgage and 1040 numbers from scratch each time). Scope this narrowly: persist inputs, don't rebuild the calculator.
5. **Replace the Closer's blank "type it yourself" real-call screen with a static CLOSER/AAA cue-card, not an AI pipeline.** `runRealCall()` today is admittedly "a bare call-timer... the rep tracks BANT/signals/notes manually" — the flagship "AI listens and coaches you live" experience (Twilio → Deepgram → Claude) only exists for the one scripted training call and doesn't run for real prospects. Rather than building that pipeline (see scope-creep flag below), put Hormozi's CLOSER steps and AAA objection prompts on-screen as a lightweight, static reference panel next to the manual BANT fields. Zero telephony/ASR integration required, and it directly upgrades how a rep runs the call they're already having today.

## Scope-creep flags
- **The last ~13 consecutive CHANGELOG entries (of ~50 total commits, all within one week) are Epic Life Ledger work, and it's now 58% of all recent commits** — chronic-illness rider redesign, Monte Carlo upgrades, a printable report, a new wizard — while the sales-facing tools sat with the outcome-logging bug (#1 above), the unrouted-referral gap (#2), and fake production numbers (#3) unfixed. This is the exact pattern flagged in Bruce's brief: a real, well-built tool absorbing the week's entire engineering effort while three bounded, high-leverage sales-plumbing bugs went untouched. None of the Ledger work this week was necessarily wrong to do, but it wasn't the highest-leverage thing available.
- **Building the full live-transcription AI co-pilot (Twilio audio → Deepgram → Claude → UI) is a multi-week, high-technical-risk build** (real-time ASR latency, telephony integration, prompt reliability under live pressure) that is not required to run or close real calls today — reps can already work the manual BANT fields. Recommendation #5 above (a static cue-card) captures most of the coaching value at near-zero build cost; treat the full AI pipeline as a later, explicitly-scoped project gated behind #1–#4 landing and Bruce running calls on the simpler version first. Building it now, before the data-loss bug is fixed, would mean layering a sophisticated new feature on top of a funnel that's still silently dropping the deals it's meant to help close.
- **Beta's Ledger development has moved outside this repo entirely** (the two most recent commits mirror in a "Leverage Game restructure" from an external "alice-tla staging" repo, with no corresponding CHANGELOG entries here). Worth Bruce explicitly confirming this is intentional rather than a second parallel tool-building surface opening up unnoticed.
- `DEPLOY.md` is stale (references old filenames/URLs that no longer exist) — low-risk, not worth fixing tonight, noted only so it doesn't cause a bad promotion later.

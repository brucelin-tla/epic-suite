### Dan Martell — Buyback/automation frameworks (moderate-high confidence, WebFetch blocked, cross-checked search snippets)

- **Buyback Rate**: (annual income ÷ 2,000 hours) ÷ 4 = the hourly rate below which a task should be delegated/outsourced. Doing sub-Buyback-Rate work yourself makes you, in his words, "the most expensive administrative assistant in your company." — [Forbes, Jan 2025](https://www.forbes.com/sites/omaidhomayun/2025/01/22/prison-to-prosperity-the-man-who-learned-to-master-time/), [Teamly](https://www.teamly.com/blog/buy-back-your-time-by-dan-martell-chapter-2/)
- **DRIP Matrix** (his actual delegation framework, not literally "DAE"): Delegation (low money/low energy — automate first), Replacement (high money/low energy — replace yourself next), Investment (low money/high energy — protect), Production (high money/high energy — your zone of genius, keep). — [Rise with Drew](https://risewithdrew.com/the-four-quadrants-of-time-how-to-maximize-energy-and-income-with-dan-martells-buyback-matrix/)
- **Replacement Ladder**: hire in this order — Admin assistant → Delivery/Support → Marketing → Sales → Leadership. Sales-role delegation/automation comes *after* admin and delivery are handled, not before. — [Martell's own LinkedIn post](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/dmartell_the-replacement-ladder-activity-7282803919276097536-gLvG)
- **3-Level AI Agent Framework** (2025-26 content, moderate confidence — secondary tutorial summary): Level 1 = AI as advisor (you still execute); Level 2 = agentic tools, you assign a goal and review output; Level 3 = an orchestrator managing sub-agents. His guidance is to move up levels sequentially, not jump straight to full autonomy. — [marketingagent.blog summary](https://marketingagent.blog/2026/04/09/tutorial-dan-martells-3-level-ai-agent-framework/)
- SMS/DM-based automated follow-up cutting no-show rates is a pattern he's used in his own business (vendor case study, treat as vendor-sourced, not independently audited): [Salesmsg case study](https://www.salesmessage.com/blog/saas-academy-case-study)

### Alex Hormozi — sales/offer frameworks (Value Equation and Core Four are well-corroborated; close-rate bands and follow-up counts are secondary-sourced only)

- **Value Equation**: Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice). His stated emphasis: most businesses over-invest in the numerator and under-invest in shrinking the denominator (speed, ease) — that's "where the magic is." — [acquisition.com/training/offers4](https://www.acquisition.com/training/offers4), [Greg Faxon's summary](https://www.gregfaxon.com/blog/100m-offers-summary)
- **Core Four** lead channels: warm outreach, warm/free content, cold outreach, cold content/paid ads. For a business with an existing customer base, his guidance is to work warm outreach first — mine the existing network for referrals with an ask like *"Do you know anyone who is facing [problem] and wants [outcome] in [timeframe]?"* — before spending on colder channels. This directly validates EPIC's Referral-Partner-first architecture. — [Shortform](https://www.shortform.com/blog/100m-leads-alex-hormozi/), [scrap.io](https://scrap.io/alex-hormozi-local-lead-generation-guide)
- **Speed-to-lead (highest-confidence single stat — direct primary quote, his own X post)**: *"I spoke to a business owner last week who gets to 100% of leads within 60 seconds. Guess what his close rate of leads was… 55%. Today's reminder to call your leads faster."* — [x.com/AlexHormozi/status/1879936850959474915](https://x.com/AlexHormozi/status/1879936850959474915)
- **CLOSER** call structure (Clarify → Label pain → expand → show outcome → handle concerns → Reinforce) and **AAA** objection loop (Acknowledge → Associate to a past similar client → Ask again) — [salescoachpro.io](https://www.salescoachpro.io/blog/hormozi-closer-framework)
- Close-rate self-diagnostic bands (30–40% healthy; below 30% = fix sales process before price; well above suggests underpricing) — secondary-sourced only, not independently verified against a primary text.
- Retention: a "Five Horsemen of Retention" churn-elimination protocol, and a cited example of churn dropping 9%→3% via a 9-step protocol; exit interviews alone reportedly save roughly half of cancellation attempts — secondary/course-summary sourced only (Scribd, podcast-summary), not book-verified.

## Concrete next steps for EPIC Suite — ranked by what most directly speeds up closing real calls today

1. **Fix Admin's fake dashboard data on root/live.** Right now, if anyone opens the live Admin dashboard mid-sales-day, they see fabricated numbers (42 referrals, fictional rep names) instead of the real figures already hand-entered on beta. Per DEPLOY.md's own promotion process, this looks like a near-zero-build "promote beta's admin data to live" fix — highest leverage-per-effort item found tonight, and it's a trust/credibility risk (a rep or Bruce making a call while looking at made-up numbers) not just cosmetic.
2. **Verify/add real-time notification when a new referral or lead lands, so a Setter can call within ~60 seconds.** This is Hormozi's single best-sourced, highest-leverage stat (100%-within-60-seconds → 55% close rate, his own words). The repo has the plumbing (real Supabase `referrals`/`prospects` tables) — the open question is whether a new row currently pushes any real-time alert to a Setter, or whether it just sits in a queue until someone happens to check. If it's poll/queue-only today, wiring a Supabase realtime subscription (or even a push notification) to alert the on-duty Setter the moment a referral lands is small, bounded, and matches the single most defensible number either researched guru gives.
3. **Persist Client Success's checklists to Supabase instead of hardcoded JS.** Client Success currently can't actually retain state between sessions — the tool doesn't do the one job it exists for. This is retention-motion infrastructure (TLA's #2 stated priority), not new scope: it's making an existing, half-built feature actually work, following the same Supabase read/write pattern already used in Referral/Setter/Admin.
4. **Route real call transcripts through the already-wired Fathom webhook instead of building the from-scratch Twilio→Deepgram→Claude real-time pipeline.** Per Martell's Replacement Ladder and 3-Level AI framework, this is the "Level 1 → Level 2" move: use an existing tool (Fathom, already partially wired in Admin) to get AI-assisted call summaries/coaching notes flowing into the Closer tool, rather than building bespoke real-time audio infrastructure that duplicates ~80% of that value at far higher engineering cost. This unlocks real (post-call) AI assistance for reps without a multi-week telephony build.
5. **Chase the setter SMS A2P carrier-review approval** (external/compliance, not a coding task) — the "Text Roadmap" SMS nurture feature is already built on beta and, per Martell's SMS-follow-up pattern (cuts no-shows) and Hormozi's Core-Four/warm-channel emphasis, is worth having live once the carrier unblocks it. This is a phone call/paperwork task for Bruce, not more building.

## Scope-creep flags

- **The real-time Twilio→Deepgram→Claude call-listening pipeline is the single biggest scope-creep risk found tonight.** It's explicitly marked as unbuilt future work in the Closer tool's own UI text, it's a multi-week telephony/streaming-audio engineering project, and — per finding #4 above — the already-half-wired Fathom integration likely delivers most of the same value (AI sees what happened on a call) for a fraction of the build cost. Recommend explicitly deprioritizing the bespoke real-time pipeline until a Fathom-based post-call flow is tried and found insufficient.
- **Martell's more speculative "5 AI agent roles" (Closer/Assistant/Workflow/Amplifier/Money) orchestration content is low-confidence-sourced** (found only via an unverified user-uploaded document) and describes a multi-agent architecture build. Not worth pursuing until items 1–4 above (fixing real data, speed-to-lead, persistence) are done — building a fancier agent-orchestration layer on top of a dashboard that still shows fake numbers would be exactly the pattern Bruce has flagged in himself: building tools instead of doing the more boring, more valuable data-plumbing and calendar-filling work.
- **Hormozi's "Sell By Chat" (closing deals via DM instead of calls) is a sales-*strategy* change, not a tooling gap** — surfacing it here only as an idea for Bruce to weigh, not something to silently build into EPIC Suite. Building DM-closing tooling before Bruce decides whether he wants to change his sales motion would be scope creep dressed as a feature.
